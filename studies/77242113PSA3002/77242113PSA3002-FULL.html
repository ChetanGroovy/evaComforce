General Study Information

We are enrolling in a Phase 3 clinical research study for adults (18 and older) with active psoriatic arthritis (PsA) who also have plaque psoriasis (or psoriatic nail changes), and whose PsA is not fully controlled by their current treatment. The study tests an investigational medicine, icotrokinra (JNJ-77242113), taken as an oral tablet once a day, compared with placebo. Both people who have and have not previously used a biologic for PsA or psoriasis may take part. The study is sponsored by Janssen. NOTE: the study drug is an ORAL TABLET taken once daily upon waking on an empty stomach — not an injection. [Specific site location and phone are not in the documents — supply from the site.]

Trial Design

Phase 3, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled, multicenter study with 3 arms randomized 1:1:1 (icotrokinra 200 mg once daily, icotrokinra 400 mg once daily, or placebo once daily). Approximately 750 participants across ~210 sites. The main study is about 52 weeks: a 5-week screening period, a 16-week blinded placebo-controlled period, and a 36-week blinded active-treatment period; participants on placebo cross over to active icotrokinra at Week 16. There is an optional 52-week blinded long-term extension (48 weeks active treatment + 4 weeks safety follow-up), for a total participation of up to 2 years. A safety follow-up visit occurs about 4 weeks after the last dose for those not entering the extension.

Compensation / Reimbursement

There is no payment for taking part in this study. Participants are reimbursed $66.00 per study visit for travel/expenses, and a caregiver who accompanies the participant is reimbursed $20.00 per visit. The site will explain how these reimbursements are paid.

Blinding

Double-blind — participant, investigator, and sponsor are blinded. Icotrokinra and placebo tablets are identical in labeling and appearance to maintain the blind. An Independent Data Monitoring Committee oversees safety; selected sponsor staff are unblinded at the Week-24 and Week-52 data cuts for analysis.
