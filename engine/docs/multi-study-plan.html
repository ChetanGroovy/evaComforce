Comforce.Engine · next stage · 18 Jun 2026

Today: the engine screens one patient against one study (WC45276), happy path only.
Next: drop in any number of studies as data; a patient is screened against all the studies they're a candidate for at once; if they don't fit the main one but fit another, we say "you may qualify for X and Y" instead of rejecting them — and we handle the two other outcomes (didn't-qualify, not-interested).

② The glue: shared fact tags

Right now each study's questions are named per-study (q1, q2…) and don't know that another study's "age" question is the same thing. To link studies, every question gets a canonical fact id. That's the one change to the question-bank format:

{ "id": "q1",
  "fact": "age",                // ◀ NEW — shared across ALL studies
  "text": "What is your age?",
  "type": "number",
  "polarity": "requirement",
  "disqualify_if": "age < 18" }
Why this is the whole trick: the patient answers age = 52 once. That single fact is applied to every candidate study's age rule at the same time. Demographics (age, sex, BMI) are asked once for the whole portfolio; only study-specific questions (e.g. "do you have type 2 diabetes?") get asked when their study is still in the running. This is exactly how Eva's kernel already works — we reuse it.

③ Screening fan-out — one answer, all studies

The patient (e.g. Albert) is a candidate for several studies. Each answer updates every study's status at once. Studies fall away as they fail; whoever survives is a match. (Illustrative.)

Open questions to settle

  • How is a patient's candidate study list decided — by indication/category, by the outreach campaign, or all active studies?
  • When two studies both survive, do we book the highest-priority one and offer the other, or ask the patient?
  • Do different studies' "same" question ever differ in threshold (e.g. BMI ≥27 vs ≥30)? → the fact is shared but each study keeps its own rule (already handled — rules are per-study).
  • Should reroute happen live mid-chat ("let's switch to the heart study") or only at the end as a summary?

Plan · the kernel stays deterministic and auditable; the LLM only phrases + understands · everything the engine decides is reviewable, and study question sets are human-approved before they gate patients.
